About Me

I'm Shlok, a software developer who believes accessible technology shouldn't be an afterthought. Living with amblyopia has taught me that good design means everyone gets to participate.

Accessibility isn't just a feature—it's a fundamental right. I'm here to build digital experiences that actually work for all humans, not just the "average" user that doesn't exist.

02

What I Care About

A

Visual Accessibility

Color contrast, scalable type, screen readers, alt text—these aren't checkboxes. They're the difference between someone using your site or leaving frustrated. Having amblyopia myself means I test visual accessibility every day just by existing online.

B

Inclusive Development

Semantic HTML, keyboard nav, proper ARIA—I build this stuff from day one. Not because it's required, but because excluding people from the web is just lazy engineering.

C

Tech Experimentation

My homelab isn't just for fun—it's where I test if new tech is actually usable for everyone. Breaking things and rebuilding them accessibly is how we move forward.
